Introduction

Made in China?

For many large Chinese companies, the opportunity for future growth lies in overseas markets. In an environment of economic conservatism and increased volatility in global markets, it is more important than ever for Chinese brands to remain connected, open, and innovative in the global marketplace.

However, for too long 'Made in China' has been labeled as 'low-end, cheap quality', and how to expand overseas is one of the big questions for many Chinese technology companies. Proving Chinese technology and innovation to overseas markets is an arduous process. Very often, the self-perception of a Chinese brand is very different from the perception of that brand in an international context.


Photo by Carl Nenzen Loven on Unsplash

So, what can be done to bridge this perception gap in China's technology manufacturing industry? What can be done to successfully reverse the stereotype of "Made in China"? Chinese technology companies such as Xiaomi have one thing in common on this issue: they use the language of their target audience to communicate their brand message. Many Chinese brands have realized that it is more effective to leverage specific user groups with global reach than to reach the general public. In today's digital age, Chinese companies looking to reach digital millennials must start with global social media platforms.

Twitter is undoubtedly the most influential social media platform in the world today and a platform for people to learn about global trends and join the discussion on hot topics. As the world's largest public conversation platform, Twitter provides a powerful channel for Chinese brands to keep their finger on the pulse of overseas consumer trends in real time. For Chinese brands aspiring to win in overseas markets, Twitter provides a powerful platform to connect with the most influential and receptive high-value audiences overseas.

This report will analyze data from the Twitter operation of Xiaomi, one of China's high-tech brand companies, to show how such a Chinese brand can successfully connect with influential overseas audiences through Twitter's unique global real-time engagement platform.

Data Acquisition

Data Acquisition

Twint (data)


Some of the data in this article were obtained using Twint. It's an advanced Twitter scraping & OSINT tool written in Python that doesn't use Twitter's API, allowing you to scrape a user's followers, following, tweets, and more while evading most API limitations. By learning how to use Twint, I scraped 2483 tweets with ‘#mi11’ between Dec 23rd and Dec 30th, including ‘username’, ‘tweet’, ‘geo’, ‘created_at’, ‘hashtags’ and ‘retweet’. This data is for reference only for the selection of co-hashtags, as the data has some issues and I cannot fix them at the moment, for example, the "geo" list is empty and there are some strange characters in the tweet text. A pivot table of the data reveals the use of hashtags related to this topic. The number of tweets using only "mi11" and using both "mi11" and "Xiaomi" has a clear advantage over other hashtags, which directly helps my choice of co-hashtags: '#xiaomi OR #mi11'.


TAGS v6.1 (data)


Because of the shortcomings of Twint for this data collection, I have also chosen to use TAGS to help with the data collection. The other part of the data came from TAGS v6.1. As TAGS has a limit on the number of tweets collected, I set up hourly updates to the archive to capture a higher amount of data than the limitation. From 23 December to 30 December, 15625 pieces of data were collected.

Location of tweets and profiles


The tweets mostly were from India, ‘from the cloud’, France, Brasil and Indonesia. There are 9,623 users whose profiles have a location, which is about 61.6% of all users. But there are only 45 tweets with geo-coordinates, which is about 0.2% of all tweets.

Here is an interesting phenomenon: the largest user group comes from India. Even though the Indian government recently banned Chinese mobile phone applications and China and India had frictions in the border areas, Xiaomi is still the best selling mobile phone brand in the Indian online market.



Histogram of tweeters volumes


We can also find that 6 person tweeted more than 100 times, 15 people tweeted 50-100 times, 176 people tweeted 10-49 times, 217 people tweeted 5-9 times, 382 people tweeted 3-4 times and 6340 people tweeted 1-2 times.



Demographic groupings


There are two indexes to discuss the information influence, the amounts of followers and the amount of users' friends. There were 2 users which had more than 10 million followers and more than two thousand users had 100-999 followers.


The amounts of users' friends

After statistics, it is found that the top three people in terms of number of friends are: @Ebox_Support, @chollistas and @IamAyan_Sarkar. The number of their friends are all over 100,000.


The amounts of followers

The tweeter who has the largest number of followers is @detikcom with 98,886,697 followers. Detikcom is an Indonesian digital media company. So it's very obvious that in the process of marketing in target countries, Xiaomi chose local authoritative social media accounts for promotion.
